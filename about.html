About Bird Sound ID

Bird Sound ID is a tool to help you learn and identify southern African bird calls. It includes several modes, each designed for a different stage of the learning process.

Modes

Single Bird Select

Browse and listen to individual birds one at a time. Use this mode to familiarise yourself with a specific bird's call. You can filter by species group (e.g. Cisticola, Kingfisher, Lark) to quickly find related birds, or scroll through the full list. Each bird shows a dynamic spectrogram, audio player, and a photo (if available).

Learning Mode

Play through a filtered playlist of birds. You can filter by bird group (e.g. Cisticolas, Raptors, Seedeaters), hotspot (birding location), habitat, and difficulty level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced). The playlist plays each bird in sequence, showing the name, spectrogram, and audio. Great for systematic study before a birding trip.

Game Mode

Test your knowledge. A bird call plays and you must choose the correct answer from four options (A, B, C, D). Answers are randomised, so you cannot simply memorise positions. You can filter by group, hotspot, or level. At the end of a round, birds you answered incorrectly are listed so you can focus on them.

Soundscape ID Training

Listen to real-life field recordings containing multiple bird species. Try to identify each bird you hear in the soundscape. This mode simulates the actual birding experience where many birds call simultaneously.

Filters Explained

Species (Single Bird Select)

The species filter groups birds by the first word of their English name (e.g. all Cisticola species, all Kingfisher species). This makes it easy to compare similar-sounding birds within a family.

Bird Group

Birds are organised into broad groups such as Cisticolas, Garden and Woodland, Groundbirds, LBJs (Little Brown Jobs), Raptors, Seedeaters, Waders, and more.

Hotspot

Filter by birding location to study the birds you are likely to encounter at a specific site, for example Kruger National Park, Isimangaliso, Drakensberg & Sani Pass, Caprivi/Chobe, etc.

Habitat

Filter by habitat type (Forest, Woodland, Grassland, Savanna, Wetland, Fynbos, etc.) to focus on birds typical of a specific environment.

Level

  • 1 Beginner – Common, distinctive calls that are easy to recognise.
  • 2 Intermediate – Calls that require some experience to identify.
  • 3 Advanced – Subtle, uncommon, or easily confused calls for experienced birders.

Spectrograms

Each bird displays a scrollable spectrogram – a visual representation of the sound. Time runs along the horizontal axis and frequency (pitch) along the vertical axis. Brighter colours indicate louder sounds. The red line tracks your playback position. You can click anywhere on the spectrogram to jump to that point in the recording.

Language

You can switch between English and Afrikaans bird names using the language selector on each page.

Tips for Learning Bird Calls

Tip: Start with Beginner level in Learning Mode or Game Mode. Once you are confident with those birds, move to Intermediate, then Advanced.
Tip: Use the species filter in Single Bird Select to compare similar species side by side (e.g. all Cisticolas, all Robin-chats).
Tip: Before a birding trip, filter by the hotspot you plan to visit so you know which birds to expect.
Tip: Pay attention to the spectrogram shape – over time you will learn to recognise certain patterns even before you identify the sound.
